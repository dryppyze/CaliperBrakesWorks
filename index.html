FAQ

Quick answers before you text us your questions.

Can I wait in the garage while my brakes are serviced?

For safety and insurance reasons, the active workspace is restricted. Most Mahogany customers choose to drop off the vehicle, take a walk around the wetlands or grab coffee nearby, work remotely and text when they are ready for pickup, or arrange a simple key handoff or drop-back if they live close by.

What kind of warranty is included with my service?

All parts we source come backed by their standard manufacturer's warranty. Our rates cover installation labour only and do not include a standalone labour warranty. If a part fails down the road due to a component defect, the manufacturer covers the replacement part, but new labour is required to install it.

Is your private garage fully licensed?

Yes. The garage is a fully registered, insured automotive service business. Your vehicle is covered under garage liability insurance while it is in our care.

How do I get an estimate?

Text or call with year, make, model, package choice, front or rear pair, and whether you want supply + install or you are bringing parts.

Where do I drop off my vehicle?

The garage is near Mahogany Masters Row SE. The exact unit number is provided during text or phone coordination.

Do I need an appointment?

Yes. Drop-off is by appointment only. No appointment is confirmed until the estimate, timing, and parts route are approved.

How long does service take?

Most brake packages are same-day service and usually take 2-4 hours. Oil-change-only service, when offered, is about 20 minutes.

Can I wait while the work is done?

Sometimes. Depending on the schedule, you can either drop off and pick up later or wait in or around the vehicle until the job is done.

What does one brake pair mean?

One brake pair means both front wheels or both rear wheels. Front pair and rear pair are quoted separately unless both are requested.

What happens with supply + install parts?

The parts cost is paid upfront before ordering. Parts are then ordered and delivered by the supplier before the appointment.

What if I bring my own parts?

Fitment and compatibility are at your own risk and expense. Wrong, missing, unsafe, or poor-fit parts can pause the job, add labour time, or require rescheduling.

Will the bill change after I drop off?

No added work happens without approval. If inspection finds something different, the work pauses and you approve the revised scope first.

Will you tell me if I do not need rotors?

Yes. The goal is to do what actually needs work, not push extra repairs. Rotor replacement depends on condition, thickness, rust, grooves, and vibration.

Why does the estimate move by vehicle?

Parts cost, rotor size, vehicle weight, electronic parking brake setup, rust, seized hardware, oil type, and oil capacity can all affect the estimate.

What vehicles or jobs are not handled?

German vehicles, brake lines, ABS hydraulic issues, major suspension damage, wheel bearings, transmission, A/C, tire mounting, balancing, and no-start repairs are not booked through this page.
